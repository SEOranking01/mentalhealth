Mental Health Therapy Singapore

Getting Started with Therapy in Singapore

If you’re new to mental health therapy in Singapore, begin by identifying your goals, such as managing stress, improving mood, or navigating life transitions. You can book directly with clinics or platforms without a referral, and the first session typically covers your history, concerns, and preferred outcomes. Expect a discussion of confidentiality and consent, along with a collaborative plan for session frequency.

Choosing the Right Therapist in Singapore

Consider fit across factors like therapeutic approach, language, cultural understanding, and scheduling convenience. Review practitioner profiles and verify relevant training and experience through clinic websites or recognised professional bodies. It’s common to try an initial session and assess comfort and rapport before committing to ongoing work.

In-Person and Online Therapy Options Across the Island

Singapore offers both clinic-based sessions and secure teletherapy, giving flexibility for busy routines and privacy needs. Many providers operate in central districts and heartland neighbourhoods, while online care reduces travel time and expands choice. Some practices offer hybrid arrangements so you can alternate between in-person and virtual sessions as your needs change.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I access mental health therapy in Singapore?
You can self-book directly with private psychologists, counsellors, or psychiatrists—no referral needed. For subsidised care at public hospitals or polyclinics (including IMH), a GP/polyclinic referral is usually required. Many providers also offer secure online therapy. If you’re in crisis or at immediate risk, call 995 or go to the nearest A&E.
What types of therapy are available?
Common options include cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT), psychotherapy, counselling, trauma-focused therapy (e.g., EMDR), couples and family therapy, child and adolescent therapy, group therapy, and psychiatric care (medication management). Services are offered in public institutions, private clinics, and via telehealth.
How much does therapy cost and is it covered?
Private counselling/psychology sessions typically range from about S$120–S$250+ per 50–60 minutes; psychiatrist first consults often start around S$180–S$400+, with lower rates for follow-ups. Public institutions may offer subsidised rates for eligible Singapore Citizens/PRs. Some employer benefits and insurance (including certain Integrated Shield Plans) may cover portions of care—always confirm fees and coverage with your clinic and insurer in advance.
