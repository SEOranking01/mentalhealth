Frequently Asked Questions

What services do private mental health clinics in Singapore typically offer?
They commonly provide psychiatric assessments and diagnosis, medication management, individual psychotherapy (e.g., CBT, DBT), couples/family therapy, and child & adolescent services. Many also offer trauma-focused care, ADHD/ASD evaluations, and telehealth for suitable cases.
Do I need a referral, and are fees covered by insurance or Medisave?
You usually don’t need a referral to book; you can contact the clinic directly. Fees vary by clinician and session type; many clinics accept or can work with private insurance, but direct billing depends on insurer panels. Medisave is generally not applicable for private outpatient psychiatric or psychology sessions—confirm coverage with the clinic and your insurer.
Should I see a psychiatrist or a psychologist in Singapore, and what’s the difference?
A psychiatrist is a medical doctor who can diagnose mental health conditions, prescribe medication, and manage complex or co‑occurring issues. A psychologist provides evidence‑based psychotherapy and psychological testing but does not prescribe medication. Many clients benefit from coordinated care; if unsure, an initial assessment can help direct you to the right professional.
