Frequently Asked Questions

What services does a Mental Health Clinic in Singapore typically provide?
Comprehensive psychiatric and psychological care, including diagnostic assessment, medication management, individual psychotherapy (e.g., CBT), couples/family therapy, child and adolescent services, psychological testing, crisis support and safety planning, and telehealth/teletherapy where appropriate.
How do I book an appointment and do I need a referral?
Private clinics usually accept self-referrals; book via phone or online. For public hospitals/polyclinics, a GP or polyclinic referral can reduce wait times and unlock subsidies. Bring your NRIC/FIN, medication list, and past records. If there’s immediate risk, call 995 for emergency ambulance, the IMH Mental Health Helpline at 6389 2222, or Samaritans of Singapore at 1767 (24/7).
What are the costs and insurance/subsidy options in Singapore?
Fees vary by provider and service; contact the clinic for exact quotes. Public institutions offer means-tested subsidies with a valid referral. Selected mental health conditions under the Chronic Disease Management Programme may be claimable via MediSave (MediSave500/700) and CHAS at participating clinics. MediShield Life and Integrated Shield Plans typically cover inpatient psychiatric care and may cover selected outpatient treatments; check your policy and benefits.
