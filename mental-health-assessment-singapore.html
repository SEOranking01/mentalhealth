Mental Health Assessment Singapore

Mental Health Assessment in Singapore: What It Involves

In Singapore, a mental health assessment typically includes a conversation about mood, sleep, stressors, and daily functioning. Clinicians may use validated screening tools and review medical history to rule out physical causes. Risk and safety are discussed sensitively, with a focus on immediate needs. The outcome is a shared plan that may include monitoring, therapy options, or referrals within Singapore’s care network.

Where to Get Assessed in Singapore

You can start with a GP or polyclinic for an initial review and referral to public hospitals or specialist clinics. Private psychiatrists, psychologists, and community services also provide assessments, with appointment availability varying by provider. Residents may access subsidised care at public institutions subject to eligibility; ask about fees when booking. Many providers offer in-person and telehealth assessments to suit schedules.

Preparing for Your Appointment in Singapore

Before your appointment, jot down key concerns, when they began, and any triggers or past support you’ve tried. Bring identification and relevant medical records to streamline registration in local clinics. Expect a confidential discussion aligned with professional standards in Singapore, with exceptions only for safety. You’ll leave with next steps tailored to local services, such as therapy, follow-up reviews, or community support programs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a mental health assessment in Singapore and what does it involve?
It’s a structured evaluation by licensed professionals (psychiatrist, psychologist, counsellor) to understand your symptoms, history, risks, and daily functioning. It typically includes a confidential interview, standardised questionnaires, a mental status exam, and, where relevant, basic medical checks ordered by a GP/psychiatrist, followed by preliminary findings and a care plan.
How can I book a mental health assessment in Singapore—do I need a referral?
For subsidised public care, visit a polyclinic or CHAS GP for a referral to a restructured hospital or the Institute of Mental Health. For private care, you can self-refer to private psychiatrists, psychologists, or clinics (many offer telehealth). If you’re in crisis, call 995 or go to the nearest A&E; for general guidance see https://s3.us-east-005.dream.io/therapy01/index.html
How much does a mental health assessment cost in Singapore, and is it covered?
Fees vary by setting, clinician type, and assessment complexity; public services are generally lower with subsidies, while private clinics may have shorter waits at higher fees. Some employer assistance programmes, insurance plans, and national healthcare schemes may cover part of the cost—confirm benefits with your provider and insurer before booking.
