Mental Health Services Singapore

Accessing Mental Health Services in Singapore

Start with a GP or polyclinic for an initial assessment, then get referred to specialists at restructured hospitals, the Institute of Mental Health, or private clinics as needed. Many providers offer services in multiple languages and cater to diverse cultural backgrounds. Wait times and appointment availability can vary, so consider booking early or exploring alternative providers if timelines are critical.

Community and Nonprofit Support Networks

Community organizations and nonprofits run helplines, counseling services, and peer support groups that complement clinical care. These programs often focus on youth, seniors, caregivers, and specific conditions, providing education, coping strategies, and social connection. Many centers host workshops and outreach events in neighborhoods to reduce stigma and make help easier to find.

Affordability, Telehealth, and Workplace Programs

Cost support is available through public subsidies and financial assistance schemes for eligible residents, while private insurance may cover selected treatments. Telehealth options provide convenient video consultations and follow-ups, with digital tools for screening and self-management between sessions. Workplaces increasingly offer employee assistance programs and manager training to promote early support and sustainable return-to-work plans.

Frequently Asked Questions

What mental health services are available in Singapore and how do I get started?
Services include counselling/psychotherapy, psychiatric assessment and medication, community support programmes, and online therapy. Start via a GP or polyclinic referral for subsidised specialist care, or self-book with a private psychologist or psychiatrist. For an overview of Mental Health Services Singapore and options, visit https://s3.us-east-005.dream.io/therapy01/index.html.
Do I need a referral, and what will it cost?
For subsidised rates at public hospitals/specialist clinics, a referral from a polyclinic or CHAS GP is usually required; private clinics accept self-referrals. Fees vary—public institutions are generally lower. Eligible Citizens/PRs can access subsidies and financial assistance; MediSave and CHAS may be used for approved chronic mental health conditions under the Chronic Disease Management Programme (CDMP). Check clinic eligibility and your balances.
What should I do in a mental health crisis in Singapore?
If there is immediate danger, call 995 or go to the nearest A&E. For urgent emotional support, contact Samaritans of Singapore (SOS) at 1767 (24/7) or the Institute of Mental Health’s Mental Health Helpline at 6389 2222.
