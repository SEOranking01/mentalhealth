Mental Health Care Singapore

Overview of Mental Health Care in Singapore

Singapore offers a coordinated network of services spanning primary care, specialist clinics, and community partners. The Institute of Mental Health works alongside public hospitals and private providers to deliver assessment, treatment, and ongoing support. Multilingual professionals and culturally attuned programmes help meet the needs of a diverse population. Preventive education and early intervention are increasingly prioritised.

Accessing Care and Referral Pathways in Singapore

Residents commonly begin by speaking with a general practitioner or at a polyclinic, where concerns are assessed and referrals can be made to specialists when needed. Counselling and psychotherapy are available through hospital-based clinics, community organisations, and private practices, with tele-consult options for added convenience. Care teams coordinate follow-up plans to ensure continuity and timely reviews. Confidential, person-centred approaches are emphasised throughout the journey.

Community Support and Wellbeing Initiatives

Nationwide initiatives promote mental wellbeing, reduce stigma, and encourage help-seeking across workplaces, schools, and neighbourhoods. Peer support groups and community hubs offer safe spaces to share experiences and learn practical coping strategies. Employers increasingly adopt wellness policies and training to foster supportive environments. Public campaigns and workshops provide skills for stress management, resilience, and caring for others.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I get mental health care quickly in Singapore, and what should I do in a crisis?
If you or someone is in immediate danger, call 995 or go to the nearest A&E. For 24/7 crisis support, call Samaritans of Singapore (SOS) at 1767 or the IMH Mental Health Helpline at 6389 2222. For non-urgent help, book a GP or polyclinic visit for assessment and subsidised referral to public services (e.g., IMH, restructured hospitals), or self-refer to private clinics and online therapy platforms such as https://s3.us-east-005.dream.io/therapy01/index.html.
Do I need a referral to see a psychiatrist or psychologist in Singapore?
For subsidised care at public hospitals and IMH, a GP or polyclinic referral is usually required; without it, you’ll be billed as a private patient. Private psychiatrists, psychologists, and counsellors accept self-referrals. Youths can also contact CHAT (Community Health Assessment Team) for assessment and navigation support.
How much does mental health care cost in Singapore, and what subsidies or insurance can I use?
Fees vary by provider and urgency; public subsidised care generally costs less than private. You may use CHAS subsidies at participating GPs, MediSave for approved outpatient mental health under the CDMP, MediShield Life for inpatient psychiatric care (and Integrated Shield Plans for higher limits), and employer benefits or EAPs where available. Ask the clinic about means-tested subsidies and claim limits before your visit.
