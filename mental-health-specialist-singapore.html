Mental Health Specialist Singapore

Mental Health Specialist Singapore: Compassionate, Confidential Care

Our mental health specialists in Singapore provide compassionate, confidential support from your first consultation onward. You’ll receive a thorough assessment, clear next steps, and space to share your goals at your own pace. We prioritize privacy and cultural sensitivity so you feel safe and understood. Whether you’re new to care or continuing treatment, you’ll be guided every step of the way.

Evidence-Based Therapies Tailored to Singapore’s Needs

Treatment draws on evidence-based approaches such as cognitive behavioral strategies, mindfulness-informed skills, and medication reviews when appropriate. Plans are tailored to your needs, preferences, and language, with measurable goals you can track. Regular check-ins ensure progress remains aligned to what matters most to you. Your clinician explains options plainly so you can make informed choices.

Convenient Access to Care Across Singapore

Access support through centrally located clinics and secure telehealth anywhere in Singapore. Same-week appointments, evening slots, and discreet reminders help fit care into busy schedules. Getting here is simple via MRT and bus, and rescheduling is easy when life changes. You can choose the format that best suits your comfort and routine.

Team-Based Support from Psychiatrists, Psychologists, and Counselors

Care is coordinated across psychiatrists, clinical psychologists, and counselors so you don’t have to navigate alone. With your consent, we collaborate with GPs or schools to streamline information and reduce repetition. A dedicated care coordinator helps align appointments and goals. This integrated approach keeps treatment cohesive and efficient.

Workplace and Student Wellbeing in Singapore

We support stress management, anxiety relief, mood stabilization, and sleep improvement with practical, skills-based strategies. Specialists also address workplace pressures, performance blocks, and study-related challenges common in Singapore’s fast-paced environment. Preventive check-ins and psychoeducation build resilience before issues escalate. The aim is sustainable wellbeing that strengthens daily life at home, school, and work.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does a mental health specialist in Singapore do, and who should I see—psychiatrist, psychologist, or counsellor?
Psychiatrists are medical doctors who diagnose conditions and can prescribe medication; psychologists provide assessments and evidence-based therapies; counsellors/therapists offer talk therapy and coping skills. If you have severe symptoms, safety risks, or need medication, start with a psychiatrist; for therapy-focused support (e.g., anxiety, depression, stress, relationships), see a psychologist or counsellor; many people benefit from a combined approach.
Do I need a referral to see a mental health specialist in Singapore, and how do costs and subsidies work?
Private clinics usually accept self-referrals; public hospitals and specialist centres often require or prioritise GP/polyclinic referrals, which can reduce wait times and unlock subsidies. Fees vary by provider, session length, and setting; public care is typically lower with MOH subsidies, while private offers faster access at higher fees. Insurance coverage and MediSave use depend on your plan and service type; check with your insurer and provider before booking.
What should I do if I need urgent mental health help in Singapore?
If there is immediate danger to yourself or others, call 995 or go to the nearest hospital A&E (including IMH Emergency Services). For non-emergencies, book an urgent GP/polyclinic visit or contact a 24/7 crisis helpline (e.g., Samaritans of Singapore), then arrange follow-up with a mental health specialist.
