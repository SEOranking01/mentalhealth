Burnout Treatment Singapore

Personalized Burnout Treatment in Singapore

Access evidence-based support from clinicians who understand Singapore’s fast-paced environment. Your plan may include stress-focused psychotherapy, skills-based coaching, lifestyle adjustments, and coordination with medical care when appropriate. We monitor progress with clear goals and regular check-ins, so you can restore energy, sleep, and focus. Private, culturally attuned care is available across public and private settings.

Workplace-Focused Recovery Plans for Singapore Professionals

Burnout often shows up at work, so we help you redesign routines and expectations without derailing your career. Our team can advise on boundary-setting, workload conversations, and graded return-to-work steps suited to Singapore workplaces. You’ll learn practical tools for concentration, emotional regulation, and sustainable productivity. Where helpful, we can liaise with HR or supervisors while maintaining strict confidentiality.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the common signs of burnout and when should I seek help?
Persistent exhaustion, cynicism or detachment from work, reduced performance, sleep problems, frequent headaches or illnesses, irritability, and feeling ineffective are common signs. Seek help if symptoms last two weeks or more, disrupt work or relationships, or come with anxiety or low mood. If you feel unsafe or at risk of self-harm, call 995 or go to the nearest A&E in Singapore.
What does burnout treatment in Singapore involve?
Burnout treatment in Singapore typically includes evidence‑based psychotherapy (such as CBT or ACT), stress‑management and coping skills, sleep and lifestyle coaching, mindfulness, boundary‑setting and workload planning, and, when indicated, medication for co‑occurring anxiety or depression prescribed by a psychiatrist. Care is available through private clinics, restructured hospitals (e.g., IMH) via polyclinic referrals, Employee Assistance Programmes, and telehealth providers, with confidential, culturally sensitive support.
How much does burnout treatment cost in Singapore and do I need a referral?
Private counselling/therapy sessions are typically about S$80–S$250+ per 50–60 minutes; psychiatrist consultations are around S$150–S$400+ (first visits may be higher). Public institutions offer subsidised rates for eligible Singapore Citizens/PRs, usually accessed via a polyclinic referral. No referral is needed for private clinics; public specialist clinics generally require one. Some employer EAPs and insurance (e.g., certain Integrated Shield Plans) may reimburse; MediShield Life mainly covers inpatient care, and MediSave is generally not claimable for outpatient psychotherapy—confirm with your insurer. Many people see results over 6–12 sessions; private wait times are often days, public wait times can be weeks.
